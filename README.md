# PTL-Based Low Power High Speed Multiplier (GPDK 45nm)

## Overview

This project implements the research work described in the paper:

**"Low-Power, High-Speed, and Area-Efficient Multiplier Based on the PTL Logic Style"**

The objective of this project is to design and analyze an **8-bit signed Modified Booth Multiplier** using **Pass Transistor Logic (PTL)** and compare its performance with a conventional **CMOS implementation**.

The design focuses on improving:

- Power consumption
- Area efficiency
- Propagation delay

The circuits were implemented and simulated using **Cadence Virtuoso with the GPDK 45nm CMOS process**.

---


# Tools and Technology

- Cadence Virtuoso EDA Suite  
- GPDK 45nm CMOS PDK  
- Transient simulation for verification  
- Supply Voltage: **0.9 V**

# Project Objectives

The primary objectives of this project are:

- Implement **PTL-based logic cells**
- Design an **8-bit Modified Booth Multiplier**
- Construct the **adder tree and ripple carry adder**
- Compare **CMOS and PTL implementations**
- Evaluate the circuits based on:
  - Area
  - Power
  - Delay

# Multiplier Architecture

The multiplier architecture consists of three major blocks:

## 1. Modified Booth Encoder (MBE)

The Modified Booth Algorithm is used to reduce the number of partial products generated during multiplication.

Advantages of the Modified Booth algorithm:

- Reduces the number of partial products
- Improves multiplication speed
- Decreases hardware complexity
- Reduces switching activity

The encoder generates partial products based on grouped bits of the multiplier.

---

## 2. PTL Logic Cells

The design uses **custom Pass Transistor Logic cells** to reduce transistor count and improve energy efficiency.

### PTL XOR Gate (4T / 6T)

The XOR gate is heavily used in the Booth encoder.

Advantages:

- Reduced transistor count
- Lower power consumption
- Faster switching compared to CMOS XOR

---

### PTL Half Adder (12 Transistors)

The PTL half adder is used in the **adder tree** to combine partial products.

Outputs:

- Sum
- Carry

Benefits:

- Smaller area
- Reduced power consumption
- Faster operation compared to CMOS HA

---

### PTL Full Adder (16 Transistors)

The PTL full adder is used for partial product accumulation.

Function:

Sum = A ⊕ B ⊕ Cin  
Carry = (A · B) + ((A ⊕ B) · Cin)

Advantages:

- Reduced transistor count
- Lower power consumption

---

### PTL FAINV (Full Adder with Inverted Carry)

A modified PTL full adder used in the **critical path of the RCA circuit**.

Purpose:

- Improve carry propagation
- Reduce delay caused by cascaded PTL stages
- Improve critical path timing

---

## 3. Adder Tree

The adder tree combines partial products generated by the Booth encoder.

Components used:

- PTL Half Adders
- PTL Full Adders

Benefits of PTL Adder Tree:

- Reduced transistor count
- Lower switching activity
- Improved area efficiency

---

## 4. Ripple Carry Adder (RCA)

The final stage of the multiplier is the **Ripple Carry Adder**, which produces the final 16-bit multiplication result.

The RCA uses:

- PTL Full Adders
- FAINV cells
- Inverters inserted between stages to control delay and restore signal levels

# Simulation and Verification

All circuits were simulated using **Cadence Virtuoso transient analysis**.

# Performance Comparison

## Area Comparison

| Circuit | CMOS Area | PTL Area |
|-------|-------|-------|
| XOR | 4.8 µm² | 3.64 µm² |
| Half Adder | 25.45 µm² | 7.2 µm² |
| Full Adder | 61.54 µm² | 9.61 µm² |
| Multiplier | 2676.11 µm² | 2351.6 µm² |

Observation:

The PTL implementation significantly reduces the circuit area due to the lower transistor count.

---

# Power Comparison

| Circuit | CMOS | PTL |
|-------|-------|-------|
| XOR | 1.81 µW | 1.22 µW |
| Half Adder | 3.75 µW | 3.035 µW |
| Full Adder | 11.1 µW | 4.962 µW |
| Multiplier | 671.7 µW | 640.9 µW |

Observation:

PTL logic reduces power consumption due to:

- Fewer transistors
- Reduced switching capacitance

---

# Delay Comparison

| Circuit | CMOS | PTL |
|-------|-------|-------|
| XOR | 80.54 ps | 66.05 ps |
| Half Adder | 95.1 ps | 79.89 ps |
| Full Adder | 125.3 ps | 129.2 ps |
| Multiplier | 1190.5 ps | 1098.2 ps |

Observation:

Although PTL full adders slightly increase delay, the overall multiplier architecture achieves **lower total delay**.

---

# Key Results

Compared to CMOS implementation, the PTL multiplier achieves:

- Reduced transistor count
- Reduced circuit area
- Lower power consumption
- Improved overall multiplier delay

These improvements demonstrate the effectiveness of **Pass Transistor Logic in arithmetic circuit design**.
